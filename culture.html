Through history, sports have had a visible impact on society and its culture. Sports have been used for socialisation, competition, and amusement. Politics, social issues, and national identity have all been promoted through sports. They have been used to encourage harmony, teamwork and understanding and to help put aside differences between various racial and ethnic groupings. As many businesses and sectors rely on sports-related activities for their survival, sports have had a massive impact on the economy. Sports have had a significant impact on culture and society, and this impact will probably last for a very long time. Sports' influence on society is demonstrated in a number of ways through athletes' celebrity status. Athletes frequently serve as role models and cultural influencers, and their fame and success can have a profound effect on society. Athletes can utilise their fame to support charitable organisations, bring attention to pressing problems, and motivate others to succeed. Athletes can make money through sponsorships and endorsements by leveraging their celebrity status to market their own personal brands. In addition, athletes' celebrity status can serve to market the sport, boosting interest and bringing in money for the business.
