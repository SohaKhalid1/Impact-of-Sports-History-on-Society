Canadian ice hockey evolved from Northern European field hockey. Though the game has gone through many variations over time, the modern and Canadian rules that have shaped the game to what it is today were created by Canadian James Creighton before the first official game was played in 1875 at the Montreal Victoria Skating Rink. The first game contained two nine-player teams with a wooden puck to play with. Lord Stanley, a previous Governor General, was the one to inaugurate the highest level trophy in hockey, now known as the Stanley Cup, in his honor. Present time, NHL teams have a 23-player roster, with 20 on the bench for every game (4 lines of forwards, 3 pairs of defense, and 2 goaltenders) and play with a puck made of vulcanized rubber. 32 teams play 82 games a season to fight for playoff spots and then the Stanley Cup.
